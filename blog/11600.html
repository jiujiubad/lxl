How much time do you really have?

by xiaolai on 2012/02/08 · 19 comments

in Daily

Suppose we could live to be 80 years old, then we get roughly 700,800 hours to live, which is 24 hours times 365 days, and times 80 years.

700,800 hours.

Nobody gets enough, and a sad fact is, most would get much less. According the the World Bank Indicators (2010), life expectancy of female at birth in 2009 was 71.3 years, and that of male was only 67.1 years.

Even if we pretend to be optimistic, holding the expectancy as 80 years, we are still in worse predicament than expected. You see, we have to sleep. Suppose we sleep for 8 hours each day, we have to take away 233,600 hours, what remains is 467,200 hours. We have to eat, if we spend 30 minutes per meal, then we have to further subtract 43,800 hours.

Human beings are not tireless machines. Even a workaholic has his idle time, and the average idle time a person has per day is approximately 2.5 hours or more. In fact, more than 50 percent of people have an idle time of more than 5 hours. And the idle time increases dramatically at both ends of life. On average, we have to cross out 73,000 hours. By now, we have only 350,400 hours, which is equivalent to 40 years.

We still have a long list to knock off from the 40 years remained. For example, what should not be neglected is that on average an individual spends more than 30 minutes in bathroom per day, and it would be much longer if one is fastidious about personal hygiene. Bong! At least 12/3 years are further nullified. We walk, we drive, and we wait for lines or lights, all of which are time-consuming. We play, we love, we have sexes, we all enjoy spare time. We easily fall prey to bad emotions, which would put us into idle states long enough…

However, according to Malcolm Gladwell, whatever we practice for 10,000 hours would turn us into professionals, or in his term, “outliers“. It seems we have enough hours, do we? But why are outliers so rare (of cause, by definition, they should be rare.)? The answer is that most people are always wasting their time.

Important facts have it in common that sometimes only a glimpse of them might motivate viewers’ transformation. Once you’re aware of the above facts, you are initiating your own revolution, a transformation that you cannot reverse.
